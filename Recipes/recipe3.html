Ingredients

  • 1 egg
  • 3 tablespoons finely chopped fresh basil
  • 3 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 1/2 pounds ground beef, pork, and veal (equal parts)
  • 3/4 cup Italian style bread crumbs
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated parmesan
  • 32 fluid ounces marinara
  • 1 pound spaghetti

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk egg, basil, parsley, oregano, salt, pepper, garlic, and water.
  3. Add meat, breadcrumbs and cheese. Mix with hands until just combined.
  4. Roll mixture into golf ball-sized meatballs and place on ungreased baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 10 minutes, then turn meatballs and cook for another ten minutes, until they are brown and almost cooked through.
  6. Bring marinara sauce to a simmer in a large skillet.
  7. Transfer meatballs to sauce, leaving fat behind. Loosely cover and simmer for 10 minutes, until meatballs are cooked through. Keep warm.
  8. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add spaghetti and cook until al dente.
  9. Drain pasta and toss with sauce and meatballs.
  10. Serve immediately, topped with fresh basil and grated parmesan.
